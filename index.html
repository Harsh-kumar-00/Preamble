Fundamental Rights

  • Right to equality
  • Right to freedom
  • Right against exploitation
  • Right to freedom of religion
  • Cultural and educational rights
  • Right to constitutional remedies

Fundamental Duties

  • Preserve natural environment
  • Follow ideals of the freedom struggle
  • Develop scientific temper and humanity
  • Protect sovereignty & integrity of India
  • Preserve composite culture of the country
  • Developing the spirit of common brotherhood
  • Safeguard public property and avoid violence
  • Strive for excellence in all spheres of life.
  • Defend the country and render national services when called upon
  • Abide by the Constitution and respect national flag & National Anthem
  • Duty of all parents/guardians to send their children in the age group of 6-14 years to school.

Directive Principles

  • The state should ensure that social, economic, and political justice is upheld in all institutions of national life
  • The state should strive to reduce economic inequality and ensure that all employees receive a livable wage
  • The state should promote the educational and economic interests of weaker sections of society, especially Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes.
  • The state should provide free legal aid to the poor and weaker sections of society
  • The state should promote international peace and security, and maintain just and honorable relations with other nations.
  • The state should ensure that men and women receive equal pay for equal work.
  • The state should protect children from exploitation and ensure that they are given opportunities to develop in a healthy manner.
